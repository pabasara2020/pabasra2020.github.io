Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery


About the Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a refuge dedicated to sea turtle conservation and protection. We offer a secure environment for turtle eggs to hatch and care for wounded or sick turtles until they may be returned to the sea.

Visitors may learn about different turtle species, their life cycles, and the obstacles they encounter in the wild at the hatchery. We provide educational programs and guided excursions to encourage turtle conservation and responsible tourism.

You may also join in the release of newborn turtles into the sea at the hatchery, a wonderful event that helps to preserve these magnificent creatures.

Things to do

  1. Assist in Nesting or Release

    2. Depending on the activities and rules of the hatchery, you may be able to engage in nesting or release programs. This might include locating and protecting turtle nests or releasing hatchlings into the ocean. Check ahead of time with the hatchery to determine whether these activities are offered.

  2. Volunteer or Take Part in a Conservation Program

    3. Some hatcheries include volunteer opportunities or conservation programs in which visitors can actively assist in turtle conservation efforts. This might include nest monitoring, beach cleanup, or aiding with research initiatives.

  3. Investigate Exhibitions and Displays

    4. Hatcheries frequently contain instructive displays highlighting the many turtle species, their habitats, and the challenges they face. Take the time to explore these exhibitions and learn more about sea turtles.

  4. Observe the hatchlings

    5. Seeing the newborn turtles hatch from their eggs is one of the great attractions at a turtle hatchery. You can view the hatchlings making their way to the water and experience this beautiful event

  5. Learn about turtle conservation

    6. Hatcheries frequently offer educational exhibits that provide information about turtle conservation initiatives. You may learn about many species, their life cycles, and the difficulties they confront in the wild.

  6. Take a tour

    7. Many hatcheries provide guided tours led by knowledgeable staff or volunteers. These excursions can give extensive insights into the hatchery's operations, turtle nesting patterns, and turtle conservation efforts.
