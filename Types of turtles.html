Different Types of Turtles

Leatherback Sea Turtles

The leatherback turtle is the largest of all sea turtles and one of the largest reptiles on the planet,
measuring 4-8 feet in length (1.2-2.4 meters) and weighing 500-2,000 pounds (225-900 kg).
The average adult is 5-6 feet tall (1.5-1.8 m) and weighs 600-800 pounds (270-360 kg).
It is the oldest of all sea turtle species, having existed for more than 150 million years!
They survived the dinosaur extinction and thrived until the last few decades, when
human interactions took their toll.





Green sea Turtles

After the leatherback, the green sea turtle is the second biggest.
They may weigh up to 500 pounds (225 kilograms) and grow to be four
feet (1.2 meters) long. The adult is a herbivore that feeds on sea grasses,
seaweeds, algae, and other marine plant life. Their beaks are pointed and
sharply serrated, making them ideal for feeding in seagrass beds and scraping
algae off hard surfaces.This is the only species that comes to the coast to bask
on a regular basis. Basking is only found in Hawaii, namely the Northwestern
Hawaiian Island chain, the Galapagos Islands, and parts of Australia.



Hawksbill

The hawksbill sea turtle is found in tropical regions all over the world and
is considered by many to be the most beautiful of sea turtles due to its multicolored
shells.They like coral reefs, rocky places, lagoons, mangroves, oceanic islands, and shallow
coastal areas.Hawksbills, so named because of its tiny head and sharp, bird-like beak, can
reach into the cracksand crevices of coral reefs in search of food. Their diet is quite
specialized, consisting nearly entirely on sponges. Adults of this smaller turtle weigh
100-200 pounds (45-90 kg) and grow tobe 2-3 feet (5 to 1 meter) long.

Loggerhead

The loggerhead turtle is one of the largest marine turtle species, weighing
200-400 pounds (90-180 kg) and reaching lengths of up to 4 feet (1.2 meters).
They may be found in the Pacific, Indian, and Atlantic Oceans' temperate and
tropical zones.They are called for their huge head and strong crushing jaw
, which allows them to consume hard-shelled food such as crabs, conchs, and
whelks. They are one of the most numerous species found in the United States.
